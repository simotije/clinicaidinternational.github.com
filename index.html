Who are we?

ClinicAid International is dedicated to achieving the US Department of Health and Human Service’s primary goal of protecting the health of all Americans and providing essential human services, especially for those who are least able to help themselves. ClinicAid International does so by helping to improve access to medical care for those without health insurance and those without the means to pay for healthcare. The right to good health, in which everyone has the right to the highest attainable standard of physical and mental health, includes access to all medical services, sanitation, adequate food, decent housing, healthy working conditions, and a clean environment is essential to ensuring an individual’s overall wellbeing. In order to properly access medical services, a critical prerequisite of good health, hospitals, clinics, pharmaceutical drugs, and doctors’ services must be accessible, available, acceptable, and of good quality for everyone, on an equitable basis, wherever and whenever needed. However, that is not the case for many Americans. As indicated by the Human Development Index, the United States is the fourth most developed nation in the world yet our health care system ranks 37 out of 191 countries according to its performance consistent with a report by World Health Organization.

We are a tax-exempt, 501(c)(3) non-profit organization which relies upon private funding to subsist. The clinic offers a wide range of medical and pharmaceutical services at no charge. This includes full medical attention by a licensed physician, diagnostic laboratory work, and prescription medication. ClinicAid is unique in that it is student-managed through volunteer efforts. Furthermore, physicians and pharmacists volunteer their time and efforts in exchange for accredited Continuing Medical Education credits. ClinicAid International seeks to provide high-quality and comprehensive care to medically uninsured and underserved patients in the 75216, 75217, and 75241 zip codes of Dallas County, TX. These locations of South Dallas are designated as medically underserved areas/populations (MUA/MUP) and as Health Professional Shortage Areas (HPSA), thereby emphasizing the populations’ greater need for quality healthcare. ClinicAid of America will be operated as a secondary health care center providing specialty services extending to: cardiology, dermatology, endocrinology, gastroenterology, gynecology, nephrology, neurology, orthopedic surgery, podiatry, and rheumatology.
